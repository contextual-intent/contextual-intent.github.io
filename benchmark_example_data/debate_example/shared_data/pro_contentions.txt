Reducing restrictions on legal immigration fills labor shortages, increases productivity, and stimulates economic growth—especially in healthcare, STEM, agriculture, and service sectors facing chronic worker gaps.
Immigrants are disproportionately responsible for innovation, founding startups, filing patents, and advancing research; easing legal pathways helps the U.S. attract and retain global talent.
Legal immigrants contribute more in taxes than they consume in public benefits over time, strengthening Social Security, Medicare, and local tax bases—especially critical in an aging society.
Expanding legal immigration channels improves border security by reducing incentives for illegal crossings, allowing authorities to focus resources on genuine security threats.
Increased legal immigration offsets population aging and decline, revitalizes communities, and reinforces America’s identity as a nation of immigrants grounded in opportunity and pluralism.
